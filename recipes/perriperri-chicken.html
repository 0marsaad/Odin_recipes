Perri perri chicken

Peri peri chicken, also known as piri piri chicken, is a popular dish that originated in Portugal and has become a staple in many cuisines, particularly in African countries like Mozambique and Angola. The dish is named after the peri peri chili pepper, which is a key ingredient in the marinade used to flavor the chicken. The history of peri peri chicken can be traced back to the Portuguese explorers who brought chili peppers from the Americas to Africa and Asia during the Age of Discovery. The chili peppers were then incorporated into local cuisines, giving rise to the spicy and flavorful peri peri chicken. The marinade for peri peri chicken typically includes ingredients like peri peri chili peppers, lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, and various herbs and spices. The chicken is marinated in this mixture for several hours or overnight to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat. It is then grilled or roasted until it is cooked through and has a delicious charred exterior. Peri peri chicken is known for its bold and fiery flavor, thanks to the spicy peri peri chili peppers. It is often served with sides like rice, grilled vegetables, or a fresh salad. The dish has gained popularity worldwide and is loved for its unique combination of heat and tanginess. Whether you're a fan of spicy food or simply looking to try something new, peri peri chicken is a dish that is sure to satisfy your taste buds with its rich history and delicious flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 1/2 cup peri-peri sauce
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup freshly chopped parsley

Instructions

  1. Combine the peri-peri sauce, lemon juice, olive oil, and parsley in a large bowl.
  2. Add the chicken to the bowl and toss to coat.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or up to 24 hours.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Remove the chicken from the marinade and grill for 10-15 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  6. Remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for 5 minutes before serving.
