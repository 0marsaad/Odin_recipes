Steak

Steak is a popular dish made from beef. It is typically a thick cut of meat, often from the tenderloin, rib, or sirloin sections of a cow. Steak is known for its rich flavor and tender texture, making it a favorite choice for meat lovers around the world. The history of steak as a dish dates back centuries. The concept of cooking meat over an open flame has been practiced by various cultures throughout history. However, the modern concept of steak as we know it today can be traced back to the development of the cattle industry in the United States during the 19th century. With the expansion of railroads and the availability of refrigeration, the transportation of beef became more efficient, allowing for the distribution of fresh meat across the country. This led to the rise of steak as a popular dish in American cuisine. Today, steak is enjoyed in various forms and cooking styles, including grilled, pan-seared, or broiled. It can be served with a variety of accompaniments, such as vegetables, potatoes, or sauces. Whether you prefer a rare, medium, or well-done steak, it is important to cook it to your desired level of doneness to ensure both safety and taste. Remember to let the steak rest after cooking to allow the juices to redistribute and enhance the flavor. So, whether you're grilling a steak for a backyard barbecue or enjoying a fine dining experience at a steakhouse, this classic dish continues to be a favorite choice for meat enthusiasts worldwide.

  • 2 Ribeye steaks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 stick of butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Preheat the grill to high heat.
  2. Brush the steaks with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the steaks on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes on each side for medium-rare, or to your desired level of doneness.
  4. While the steaks are cooking, melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat.
  5. Add the garlic and rosemary to the butter and cook for 1-2 minutes, or until fragrant.
  6. Remove the steaks from the grill and let them rest for 5 minutes.
  7. Drizzle the garlic and rosemary butter over the steaks and serve immediately.
