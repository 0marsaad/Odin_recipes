Carbonara

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish that originated in Rome. It is known for its rich and creamy sauce made with eggs, cheese, and cured pork. The dish is typically made with spaghetti or other long pasta, and it is often flavored with black pepper. The history of carbonara is somewhat debated, but it is believed to have originated in the mid-20th century. Some theories suggest that it was created by Italian coal miners (carbonari) who used ingredients readily available to them, such as eggs, cheese, and cured pork. Others believe that it was inspired by a similar dish called cacio e ova, which dates back to ancient Rome. Regardless of its exact origins, carbonara has become a beloved dish both in Italy and around the world. Its creamy and flavorful sauce, combined with the salty and savory flavors of the pork, make it a favorite among pasta lovers. To make carbonara, you typically start by cooking the pasta until al dente. While the pasta is cooking, you whisk together eggs, cheese (usually Pecorino Romano and Parmigiano-Reggiano), and black pepper in a bowl. In a separate skillet, you cook the cured pork (usually guanciale or pancetta) until crispy. Once the pasta is cooked, it is added to the skillet with the pork, and the egg and cheese mixture is quickly tossed in to create a creamy sauce. The dish is then seasoned with black pepper and served immediately. Carbonara is a simple yet delicious dish that showcases the flavors of Italy. Whether you're a fan of traditional Italian cuisine or simply love a good pasta dish, carbonara is definitely worth trying.

  • 400 grams Spaghetti
  • 1 Egg yolk
  • 250 grams shredded Pecorino Romano
  • 100 grams shredded Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • 250 grams Guanciale (use beef bacon for a halal dish)
  • Black pepper
  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, whisk together the egg yolk, Pecorino Romano, and Parmigiano-Reggiano in a large bowl.
  3. Cook the guanciale in a large skillet over medium heat until crispy.
  4. Drain the pasta, reserving 1 cup of the pasta water.
  5. Add the pasta to the skillet with the guanciale and toss to coat.
  6. Remove the skillet from the heat and add the egg and cheese mixture, tossing quickly to coat the pasta. Add the pasta water a little at a time until the sauce reaches the desired consistency.
  7. Season with black pepper and serve immediately.
